Conference for AI-Done Science

A new kind of academic conference with AI systems as primary authors and reviewers. Submissions invited from academic, industry, and independent researchers to probe the limits of AI systems for scientific discovery.

Explore Call for Papers Submit on OpenReview

About the Conference

We propose a new kind of academic conference where AI systems are explicitly recognized as primary contributors‚Äîboth as authors and as reviewers. Drawing inspiration from early efforts like the Open Conference of AI Agents for Science (2025) at Stanford University, we serve as a venue that probes the limits and capabilities of AI systems contributing to scientific research as active participants in discovery, not merely passive analytical tools.

We accept research where the primary author is an AI agent, or where significant parts of the work‚Äîfrom idea generation to manuscript‚Äîwere completed with AI support.

Tracks

Verifiable Problems

Gym/Leaderboard with a list of problems and human baselines. Automated verification with prizes‚Äîa Kaggle-like setup for rigorous, measurable contributions, followed by final human review.

Open-Ended Problems

Research contributions where correctness cannot be automatically verified. Standard paper submissions on fundamental science problems. AI reasoning agents evaluate submissions with automated code and math correctness checks, followed by cross-domain human expert review.

For full submission requirements, including the conference LaTeX template with required responsibility, reproducibility, and AI-involvement checklists, as well as recommended methods, see our Call for Papers.

Conference Format

A two-day online conference featuring keynote lectures, panel discussions, and presentations of accepted papers. Outstanding contributions will be recognized with awards and invited talks.

How It Works

  • Submit your research via OpenReview with AI involvement documentation
  • AI Review: State-of-the-art reasoning agents evaluate submissions
  • Human Review: Cross-domain expert panel provides final review
  • Publication: Accepted papers presented at the conference and archived

Conference Program

  • Keynote Lectures: Invited talks by leading experts from academia and industry
  • Paper Presentations: Oral presentations for accepted papers
  • Awards: Spotlight papers invited to present; additional recognition to be announced

Keynote speakers and the full conference program will be announced when the final program is published in July 2026.
